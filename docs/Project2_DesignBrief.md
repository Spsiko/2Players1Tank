Game Concept and goal:
    the original concept and goal of this game was to create a tank game where two players control one tank, with one player controlling the movement of the tank and the other controlling the weapon. the goal was to create a game that embraced cooperation and was still fun.

    after revision the idea behind two players controlling one tank was difficult to pull off in a limited time frame and so the game changed fundamentally to one where its just one player attempting to survive against the enemy tanks.
Target player experience:
    <!-- the players work together to defeat enemies and survive. -->
    the player works to defeat enemies and survive to clear the levels.
Core mechanics and loop:
    the player controls a tank that drives around avoiding enemy projectiles while attempting to hit the enemies with the player's own projectiles.
    the player spawns in the level, the enemies spawn elsewhere, the player and the enemies fire at each other while navigating the level. the player either defeats the enemies and clears the level, or loses and starts over.
Scoring/progression:
    Scoring and progression are based on the player defeating the enemy tanks. If a player hits one of the tanks it is destroyed and the player is awarded points.
Collision:
    the main points of collision within the game are the bullets colliding with the player and enemy tanks and the tanks colliding with the walls of the levels. The bullets also collide with the walls of the level. 
Changes from initial idea to final build:
    as was briefly covered in the game concept and goal section the original goal was a cooperative game where the players had to work together to control the tank and clear the enemies. When put into practice this was difficult to create in a way that was entertaining for the players. This led to a fundamental change in the form of removing the cooperative element and instead focusing on the core mechanic of having the player tank and enemy tanks facing off against each other. This change enabled a better focus on creating a game that people could enjoy without clunky complex mechanics that could bring the game down.
